Why this page should not be trusted

This is a harmless offline awareness demo for a school presentation. It is built to help students spot phishing signs without collecting any information.

1

Suspicious web address

The domain is not actually google.com. Attackers often hide the real domain in a long address.

2

“Not secure” warning

A sign-in page asking for a password should never look untrusted or insecure.

3

Urgent scare tactics

Messages like “disabled today” and “30 minutes” try to make people panic instead of think.

4

Unusual sender

The fake email sender is not a normal school or Google address.

5

Unexpected password request

If you got here from a link in an email or text, entering your password could hand it to an attacker.

If this were a real phishing page, typing a password here could give an attacker access to school email, cloud files, saved passwords, and any other account that reused the same password.
Correct response: do not sign in from the link. Instead, open a new browser tab and go directly to the real website, or contact your school using a known trusted method.
Offline classroom demo. No data is collected, saved, or transmitted.
