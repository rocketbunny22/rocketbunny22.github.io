Penetration Testing

Ethical hacking services to identify vulnerabilities before malicious actors can exploit them. Simulate real-world attacks to test your defenses.

What is Penetration Testing?

Penetration testing, also known as ethical hacking, is a simulated cyber attack against your systems to check for exploitable vulnerabilities. Our certified ethical hackers use the same tools and techniques as malicious attackers to identify security weaknesses before they can be exploited.

This proactive approach helps organizations understand their real-world security posture and provides concrete evidence of vulnerabilities that need immediate attention.

Testing Types

Network Penetration Testing

Comprehensive testing of network infrastructure, including firewalls, routers, switches, and network services.

Web Application Testing

In-depth security testing of web applications, APIs, and web services to identify OWASP Top 10 vulnerabilities.

Mobile Application Testing

Security assessment of iOS and Android applications, including static and dynamic analysis.

Wireless Network Testing

Assessment of wireless network security, including WiFi, Bluetooth, and other wireless protocols.

Social Engineering Testing

Controlled testing of human factors through phishing simulations and social engineering attacks.

Physical Security Testing

Assessment of physical security controls, including access controls, surveillance, and facility security.

Our Testing Methodology

1

Pre-Engagement

Define scope, rules of engagement, and obtain proper authorization before testing begins.

2

Reconnaissance

Gather information about target systems using passive and active reconnaissance techniques.

3

Scanning & Enumeration

Identify live systems, open ports, services, and potential entry points into the target environment.

4

Exploitation

Attempt to exploit identified vulnerabilities to gain unauthorized access to systems and data.

5

Post-Exploitation

Assess the impact of successful exploits and attempt to escalate privileges or move laterally.

6

Reporting

Document all findings with proof-of-concept exploits and provide remediation recommendations.

Testing Approaches

Black Box Testing: No prior knowledge of the target system, simulating an external attacker's perspective.

White Box Testing: Full knowledge of system architecture and source code, providing comprehensive internal assessment.

Gray Box Testing: Limited knowledge of the system, simulating an insider threat or compromised user scenario.

Deliverables

Executive Summary: High-level overview of findings and business risk assessment for leadership.

Technical Report: Detailed documentation of all vulnerabilities with proof-of-concept exploits.

Remediation Guide: Step-by-step instructions for fixing identified vulnerabilities.

Risk Matrix: Prioritized list of findings based on severity and business impact.

Retest Report: Validation testing after remediation efforts are completed.
